# Research Plan: Evaluation Metrics for Automated Hypotheses

## Overview
Defining what constitutes a "good" hypothesis is critical for automated systems. This plan focuses on identifying and standardizing the metrics used to evaluate AI-generated biomedical hypotheses.

## Key Research Questions
1. What are the quantitative and qualitative metrics used to evaluate hypotheses (e.g., novelty, plausibility, testability)?
2. How can we measure "biological feasibility" in an automated way?
3. What is the correlation between AI metrics and human expert evaluation?

## Methodology
- **Metric Extraction:** Identify evaluation frameworks from recent literature (e.g., "Scientific Turing Tests").
- **Analysis:** Compare objective metrics (text similarity, KG consistency) with subjective expert scoring.
- **Framework Proposal:** Propose a standardized scoring system for biomedical hypothesis generation.

## Expected Outcomes
- A taxonomy of evaluation metrics.
- Insight into the limitations of current automated evaluation methods.
