# Research Plan: Benchmarking against Historical Discoveries

## Overview
To validate AI, we must know if it can rediscover known science. This research focuses on "back-testing" LLMs against historically significant biomedical discoveries.

## Key Research Questions
1. Can an LLM, given only data available prior to a discovery, recreate the landmark hypothesis?
2. Where do models fail in replicating the "intuitive leaps" made by famous scientists?
3. How does historical benchmarking help us trust current AI predictions?

## Methodology
- **Dataset Curation:** Create time-restricted datasets (e.g., only papers before 1990).
- **Simulation:** Prompt LLMs to generate hypotheses based on these restricted sets.
- **Gap Analysis:** Compare AI reasoning paths with historical records of scientific thought.

## Expected Outcomes
- A measure of "Scientific Reasoning Maturity" for current LLMs.
- Validation of the LLM's ability to perform inductive and deductive reasoning.
