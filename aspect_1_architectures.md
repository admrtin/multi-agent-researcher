# Research Plan: LLM Architectures for Biomedical Hypothesis Generation

## Overview
This research focuses on comparing different Large Language Model (LLM) architectures used for generating scientific hypotheses in the biomedical field. It evaluates the trade-offs between general-purpose models, domain-specific fine-tuning, and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG).

## Key Research Questions
1. How does domain-specific fine-tuning (e.g., BioGPT, Med-PaLM) compare to general models (GPT-4) in hypothesis quality?
2. To what extent does RAG improve the factual grounding of generated hypotheses?
3. What are the computational requirements for different architectural approaches?

## Methodology
- **Literature Review:** Survey existing architectures tailored for biomedical text.
- **Comparative Analysis:** Analyze benchmarks comparing zero-shot prompting vs. fine-tuned performance.
- **Gap Identification:** Identify technical bottlenecks in current model architectures for complex reasoning.

## Expected Outcomes
- A classification of LLM architectures for scientific discovery.
- Recommendations on the most effective architecture for specific biomedical sub-tasks.
